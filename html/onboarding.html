Welcome to VolumeHub

Full control of your browser's audio — volume boost, equalizer, and per-site memory in one place.

What you can do

Volume Boost up to 600%

Push quiet media as loud as you need — way beyond the browser's native limit.

3-Band Equalizer

Fine-tune bass, mids, and treble with ±12 dB sliders and handy presets.

Per-Site Memory

VolumeHub remembers your volume preference for each site and reapplies it automatically.

Default Volume

Set a fallback volume level for any site you haven't configured yet. Found in Settings.

Instant Tab Mute

Silence any audible tab in one click from the popup — all tabs, or just one.

Auto-Mute New Tabs

Turn this on in Settings to silence every new tab the moment it opens. Unmute individual tabs anytime from the popup's audio tabs list.

Backup and Restore

Export all your saved volumes and settings to a file, then import them anytime to restore or transfer to another device.

Getting started

1

Pin VolumeHub to your toolbar

Click the puzzle icon in Chrome's toolbar, find VolumeHub, and click the pin icon so it's always one click away.

2

Open any tab with audio

Navigate to a site playing music, a podcast, a video — anything with sound.

3

Click the VolumeHub icon and drag the slider

Move the volume slider up. The first capture takes ~1 second; after that changes are instant. Your setting is saved automatically for that site.

4

Explore the EQ tab

Switch to EQ to boost bass, clarify vocals with a mid lift, or roll off harshness with the treble slider. Try the presets — Bass Boost, Vocal, Night Mode.

Shortcuts worth knowing

Volume slider Scroll wheel or ↑ ↓ arrow keys to nudge volume. Hold Shift for ×10 steps.
Quick jumps Press 1–6 on the keyboard to jump straight to 100%, 200%, …, 600%.
EQ sliders Scroll anywhere in a band column, or click and drag the thumb for fine control. Shift for larger steps.
Mute all Click the mute icon in the top-right of the popup to silence every tab currently playing audio at once.

Good to know

Fullscreen on some streaming sites Some streaming sites (Prime Video, Disney+, Peacock) block the in-player fullscreen button when a browser extension is controlling audio. Press F11 instead. It works the same way.

That's all you need to know. Enjoy your audio, your way.
